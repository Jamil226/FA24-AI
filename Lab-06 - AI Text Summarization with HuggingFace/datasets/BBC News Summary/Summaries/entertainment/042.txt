South Korean film director Im Kwon-Taek has received an honorary Golden Bear at the Berlin Film Festival.Meanwhile, a film version of Bizet's opera Carmen - translated into the South African language Xhosa - has received a warm reception at the festival.U-Carmen eKhayelitsha (Carmen in Khayelitsha), which is one of 21 films up for Berlin's top prize, the Golden Bear, is British director Mark Dornford-May's first feature film.The veteran film-maker, 68, won the best director prize at the Cannes Film Festival in 2002 for Chihwaseon.
